Internal Transfer

For Guelph students and personnel, registration funds may be transferred directly from a department or lab account to Conference Services. You will need the G/L code corresponding to the account you are paying from. Ask your P.I. or department staff, as appropriate.

PayPal

PayPal can process online payments for a small fee (2.9% + $0.30). You will not be charged this fee; your total will remain the same regardless of payment method. You can checkout using a credit card via PayPal, without creating an account. Creating a PayPal account also allows you to pay using money transferred directly from a bank account.

Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a form of digital currency. Payments are processed for no or negligible fee. We use a service called Coinbase to create a Bitcoin payment button, which automatically converts our registration fee amounts from Canadian dollars to Bitcoin at current exchange rates.

If you already own Bitcoin in a Coinbase wallet, payment requires a few button clicks. If you already own Bitcoin in a non-Coinbase wallet, you may pay for registration using the following process:
   
    - After clicking "Pay with Bitcoin", select "Send using a bitcoin address".
    - Scan the QR code or copy the displayed address into your wallet.
    - A confirmation email is sent indicating receipt of payment.
    - To identify yourself as the person having submitted that payment, send a follow-up email with the first 5 digits of the public key you used to oe3c2014@gmail.com.

If you do not own any Bitcoin but want to use this method of payment, you will first need to create a digital wallet, then to obtain some Bitcoin. A variety of online exchanges allow you to purchase Bitcoin using other currencies (e.g. Canadian dollars). Coinbase is one service that functions as both a wallet and an exchange, but it unfortunately only takes funds from U.S. bank accounts. If you want more information, email our resident Bitcoin expert Zach and he will be happy to help you get started!
